**Q:** What happens in the first jhana?

**A:** In the first jhana, you might experience a significant shift from your ordinary consciousness, marked by a strong sense of rapture or joy (piti) and happiness (sukkha), which are born of seclusion from sense pleasures and unwholesome states of mind. This stage is accompanied by applied and sustained thought or examination (vitakka and vichara).

---

**Q:** Can one stumble into the higher jhanas from the first jhana?

**A:** Yes, it is possible to transition from the first jhana to higher jhanas. The practice involves deepening concentration and moving beyond the applied and sustained thought characteristic of the first jhana, allowing for a more profound state of meditative absorption.

---

**Q:** How do I enter the first jhana?

**A:** To enter the first jhana, you need to cultivate a base level of concentration strong enough to seclude you from sense pleasures and unwholesome mental states. Focus on a meditation object, such as the breath, and allow joy and happiness born of seclusion to arise. When you can maintain this with applied and sustained thought, you enter the first jhana.
